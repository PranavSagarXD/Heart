The Weight of Everything

Why I'm stripping my identity back to the core.

May 1, 26 • 2 min read

#letter

I am drowning in things I once loved.

These are just projects—things I built out of curiosity. Small ideas I enjoyed working on without worrying about where they would lead. But over time, they started to pile up. Unfinished ideas, half-complete builds, all sitting there, waiting for me to come back to them.

Now it feels like I’m trying to keep up with everything at once, and I can’t. What used to be fun has slowly turned into pressure. I used to build because I wanted to—now it feels like something I’m supposed to keep up with.

Maybe it’s because things have changed. Studies demand more time, more focus, more discipline. And somewhere in between deadlines and expectations, the space I had for creating has started to shrink.

For a while, I tried to hold onto everything—studies, projects, ideas—afraid that letting go meant losing a part of myself. But the truth is, holding onto everything is what’s exhausting me.

So I’m learning to let go.

Not because those things didn’t matter, but because I can’t carry them all right now. I’m choosing to focus, even if it means stepping away from things I once loved.

I’ll come back to them someday—maybe differently, maybe better. But for now, I need to make space for what matters in this moment.

Because sometimes, growing means knowing what to leave behind.

To those I’ve disappointed: I am wholeheartedly sorry. Your support has been my world, but I must make room for the future.

At the end of the day, letting go is the essential art of life.
